Morning facial puffiness is a common concern caused by fluid accumulation during sleep. When we lie flat, gravity allows lymphatic fluid to pool in the face rather than draining naturally. Facial cupping is one of the most effective methods for quickly moving this trapped fluid.

## Why Cupping Works for Puffiness

The lymphatic system relies on muscle movement and external pressure to circulate fluid — it has no central pump like the heart. Facial cupping provides precisely this external pressure. As you glide the cup across the skin, the suction pulls stagnant fluid from the tissue and pushes it through lymphatic channels toward the lymph nodes, where it can be filtered and eliminated.

The result is visible within minutes: reduced puffiness, particularly around the eyes, cheeks, and jawline.

## A 5-Minute Morning Routine

**Step 1 — Prepare (1 minute):** Wash your face and apply a generous layer of facial oil. The oil is essential for glide and prevents the cup from pulling at the skin.

**Step 2 — Neck drainage (1 minute):** Start with a medium cup at the base of the neck. Glide upward from the collarbone to just under the jaw in long, slow strokes. This opens the main lymphatic pathways before working on the face.

**Step 3 — Cheek decongestion (1.5 minutes):** Move to the cheeks with the same medium cup. Work from the center of the face outward — nose to ear in straight lines. Repeat 5-6 times per side.

**Step 4 — Eye area (30 seconds):** Switch to the smallest cup. Gently glide from the inner corner of the brow outward along the brow bone. Do not cup directly over the eyelid. Use the lightest possible suction.

**Step 5 — Jawline finishing (1 minute):** Use a small cup to glide along the jawbone from chin to ear. This final sweep catches any remaining fluid and defines the jawline.

## Tips for Best Results

Perform this routine before applying makeup or serum. The increased circulation will help your skincare products absorb more effectively. For persistent puffiness, consider sleeping with an extra pillow to elevate your head and reduce overnight fluid accumulation.

Avoid cupping if you have active sinus infections, as the pressure may cause discomfort. Cupping is safe to use daily for puffiness, but limit it to once per day with light to medium suction.
