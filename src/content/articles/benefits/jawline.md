Facial cupping is particularly effective for defining the jawline. The technique reduces fluid retention, releases tension in the masseter muscles, and promotes lymphatic drainage along the lower face.

## Why the Jawline Responds Well

The jaw and lower face are common areas for fluid accumulation, especially after sleep or high-sodium meals. Cupping's suction physically moves stagnant lymph toward the nodes located under the jaw and behind the ears. This immediate decongestion creates a visibly sharper jawline contour after just one session.

For people who clench or grind their teeth, the masseter muscles can become hypertrophied, creating a wider, squarer jaw appearance. The gentle suction and gliding motion of cupping helps relax these muscles over time, contributing to a softer, more defined contour.

## Technique for Jawline Definition

**Cup selection:** Use the smallest or medium cup for the jawline. Smaller cups provide more precise control along the bone.

**Direction:** Always work from the center of the chin outward toward the ear. This follows the natural lymphatic pathways of the lower face.

**Movement pattern:** Start at the chin and glide along the jawbone to the earlobe. Repeat 5-8 times per side. Then work from the corner of the mouth upward along the cheekbone in a lifting motion.

**Pressure:** Use light to medium suction on the jawline. The skin here is more mobile than on the forehead or cheeks, and excessive suction can cause bruising.

## Complementary Movements

For full facial contouring, combine jawline work with cheek lifts and brow lifts. After completing the jawline, move to the cheeks using a medium cup with upward sweeping motions from the nose toward the temple. Finish with gentle glides across the forehead to release tension.

## Expected Timeline

Immediate results from lymphatic drainage are visible after the first session — the face appears less puffy and the jawline looks more defined. Structural improvements from muscle relaxation and tissue remodeling typically appear after 4-6 weeks of consistent practice. For the best results, aim for 3-4 sessions per week as part of your morning routine.
