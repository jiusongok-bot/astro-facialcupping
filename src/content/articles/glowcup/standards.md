Our product evaluation framework is built on three pillars that guide every recommendation we make. We apply these standards consistently across all product categories, from individual cups to full sets and electric devices. Products that do not meet our minimum standards in any category are excluded from our recommendations regardless of other features.

## Material Quality

We verify material grade by reviewing product specifications, manufacturer documentation, and third-party certifications where available. For silicone cups, we look for medical-grade (USP Class VI) or food-grade silicone specifications clearly stated in the product documentation. We flag products that do not disclose their material grade as a concern requiring verification before purchase.

To assess durability, we test cups through extended use (20+ sessions), noting any changes in flexibility, surface texture, or structural integrity. We also perform a simple quality check: pinching and twisting the cup material. Low-quality silicone shows white stress marks or emits a chemical odor; quality medical-grade silicone remains translucent and odorless. We check edge finishing for burrs, rough spots, or uneven thickness that could cause discomfort or skin irritation during use.

## Usability

We evaluate cup design across several practical dimensions. Suction control is tested by assessing whether the cup allows graduated squeeze-and-release pressure rather than all-or-nothing suction. A good cup should let the user apply very light suction for delicate areas like the under-eye region and slightly firmer suction for larger areas like the cheeks and forehead.

Cup sizing is evaluated for coverage range. A quality set includes at least three sizes: large (35-45 mm) for cheeks and forehead, medium (25-35 mm) for the jawline and temples, and small (15-20 mm) for the under-eye area and around the nose. We note whether the cup size markings are clear and permanent, and whether the grip design allows comfortable handling during extended sessions.

Ease of cleaning is also scored. Cups with non-porous surfaces, wide enough openings for brush access, and dishwasher-safe materials rank higher. We check whether the cups retain oil residue or odors after washing, as this indicates material quality issues.

## Safety Framework

We assess whether products meet basic safety requirements for facial skin contact. This includes evaluating whether the suction levels produced by the cups are appropriate for facial use (not too strong), whether the edge finishing is smooth enough to prevent micro-abrasions during gliding, and whether the material is biocompatible and free from known irritants.

Products that lack basic safety information — including material specifications, cleaning instructions, and usage guidelines — are excluded from our recommendations. Products made from materials with known irritants or those that use misleading marketing (e.g., claiming "medical-grade" without certification) are also excluded.

## Evaluation Process

We purchase all products we evaluate using standard retail channels. We do not accept free samples or manufacturer-supplied units for evaluation to avoid bias. Each product is tested by at least two team members, and ratings are compared for consistency. Products are retested periodically to check for manufacturing changes.

## How Recommendations Are Made

Products must meet minimum thresholds in all three pillars (material, usability, safety) to be recommended. Within that set, we prioritize products that offer the best combination of quality and value. We clearly distinguish between products that meet our full standards and those that are adequate for beginners on a budget. No product receives a recommendation solely because of a commercial relationship, and all affiliate relationships are disclosed alongside the recommendation.
