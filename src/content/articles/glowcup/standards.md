Our product evaluation framework is built on three pillars that guide every recommendation we make. We apply these standards consistently across all product categories, from individual cups to full sets and electric devices.

## Material Quality

We verify material grade by reviewing product specifications, manufacturer documentation, and third-party certifications where available. For silicone cups, we look for medical-grade (USP Class VI) or food-grade silicone specifications. We flag products that do not disclose their material grade as a concern. We also assess durability over time through extended use testing, noting any changes in flexibility, surface texture, or structural integrity.

## Usability

We evaluate cup design, suction control, ease of use for beginners, and ergonomic considerations. Does the cup maintain consistent suction during movement? Is the grip comfortable for extended use? Are the size markings clear and permanent? These practical factors determine whether a product is genuinely useful or merely well-marketed. We prioritize products that make correct technique easier to achieve.

## Safety Framework

We assess whether products meet safety standards for skin contact, including appropriate suction levels for facial use, edge finishing quality, and material biocompatibility. Products that lack basic safety information or use materials with known irritants are excluded from our recommendations regardless of other features.
