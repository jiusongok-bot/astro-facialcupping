We believe in honest, evidence-based information that empowers readers to make informed decisions about facial cupping. Our philosophy rests on three principles that guide every article and evaluation on this site.

![Wellness therapy session](/images/articles/therapy-treatment.jpg)

## Evidence-First Approach

We present what the research says, what it does not say, and where the evidence is limited. We do not overpromise. When a claimed benefit lacks strong scientific support, we say so directly. When preliminary research is promising but inconclusive, we explain the context. This approach means some of our articles are more cautious than the marketing language you will find on product pages — and that is intentional.

We organize evidence by strength. For claims with multiple randomized controlled trials supporting them — such as cupping's effect on local blood flow — we present the data with confidence. For claims supported mainly by anecdotal reports or extrapolation from body cupping studies — such as facial collagen induction — we present the possibility while clearly noting the limitations. This graded approach helps readers understand what is well-established versus what remains promising but unproven.

## Transparency

When we recommend tools, we disclose our evaluation methodology publicly on our Product Standards page. We do not accept payment for editorial placements. Affiliate relationships, when present, are clearly labeled so readers know when a link may generate revenue for the site. Our tool evaluations follow the same standards regardless of whether a purchase link exists.

We also believe in transparent correction. If we publish something that turns out to be inaccurate, we correct it openly rather than quietly editing the text. Each correction is documented with the date and nature of the change so readers can track the site's accuracy over time.

## Accessibility

Facial cupping information should be accessible to everyone. Our content is written for beginners while remaining useful for experienced practitioners. We avoid unnecessary jargon and explain technical terms when they are unavoidable. Every article aims to answer the practical question: "What does this mean for someone using facial cupping?"

We structure our content for different reading styles. Some readers want the full evidence review; others want the practical how-to guide. Our articles are organized with clear headings and summaries so readers can find the information most relevant to them without wading through content they do not need.

## Why This Matters

Facial cupping sits at the intersection of traditional wellness practices and modern skincare. It is a field where marketing often outpaces the evidence. Our role is to bridge that gap — to provide information that is both trustworthy and useful, without the hype. We do not claim to have all the answers. What we offer is a rigorous, transparent, and reader-first approach to a practice that has genuine potential when understood and applied correctly.
