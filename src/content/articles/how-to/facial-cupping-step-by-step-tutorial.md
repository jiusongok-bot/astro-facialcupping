Follow this step-by-step guide for a complete facial cupping routine that takes approximately 10 minutes once you are comfortable with the movements.

## Step 1: Prepare

Start by thoroughly cleansing your face to remove makeup, sunscreen, and surface oils. Pat dry, then apply a generous layer of facial oil — do not skimp here. The oil should create a visible slip on the skin; you should be able to glide two fingers across your cheek without resistance. If the cup feels like it is dragging or catching during the session, pause and apply more oil immediately. Have all your cups within reach before you begin.

## Step 2: Neck and Jaw

Begin with the largest cup at the base of the neck, just above the collarbone. Use slow, upward strokes along the sides of the neck toward the jawline, working one side at a time. This clears the lymphatic pathways before you move to the face, making the facial work more effective. Spend about 1 minute on each side of the neck. Then switch to a medium cup and work along the jawline from the chin toward the earlobe in small, gliding strokes. Repeat 3-4 times per side. This area holds significant tension and benefits from extra attention.

## Step 3: Cheeks

The cheeks are the main event. Using a medium cup, start at the side of the nose and glide outward toward the ear in straight, sweeping motions. Keep the strokes slow and deliberate — rushing reduces the lymphatic effect. Work the entire cheek area, including the upper cheekbone and lower cheek near the mouth. Spend 2-3 minutes total on both cheeks. This is where many users notice the most immediate visible change in skin tone and plumpness.

## Step 4: Forehead

Switch to the largest cup for the forehead. Start at the center, just above the brows, and glide outward toward the temples. Work in horizontal rows, moving from just above the eyebrows up to the hairline. The forehead often holds tension from furrowing and frowning, so you may notice a sense of release. Spend about 1 minute on the forehead.

## Step 5: Under-Eye

Use the smallest cup with extremely light suction for the delicate under-eye area. Place the cup at the inner corner of the eye socket (near the nose) and glide gently outward along the orbital bone toward the temple. Do not pull the cup directly under the eye — work along the bone. Limit this area to 30 seconds per side maximum. If you have very thin or fragile under-eye skin, consider skipping this step entirely and focusing on the cheeks to indirectly support the eye area.
