Consistency is the single most important factor in seeing results from facial cupping. This 5-minute routine is designed to fit easily into your morning or evening skincare and covers all the key areas of the face.

## Morning Routine (5 Minutes)

**1. Cleanse face** — Always start with a clean face. Remove any sleep residue, sweat, or skincare products from the night before. Pat dry with a clean towel.

**2. Apply facial oil** — Apply a generous layer of facial oil to your entire face and neck. The oil is essential for glide and cannot be skipped. Use a clean cupping oil, jojoba oil, or your favorite facial oil that does not absorb instantly.

**3. Neck drainage (1 minute)** — Use the largest cup. Starting at the base of the neck, glide upward along the sides toward the jawline. Do both sides. This step clears the lymphatic pathways and prepares the face for treatment. Spend 30 seconds on each side with slow, upward strokes.

**4. Cheeks and jawline (2 minutes)** — Switch to a medium cup. Start at the side of the nose and glide outward across the cheekbone toward the ear. Cover the entire cheek area — upper cheek, lower cheek, and along the jawline from chin to ear. Spend about 1 minute per side. This is where most of the contouring and lifting work happens.

**5. Forehead (1 minute)** — Use the largest cup. Start at the center of the forehead above the brows and glide outward toward the temples. Work in horizontal rows from the brow line up to the hairline. This helps release tension and smooth the appearance of horizontal lines.

**6. Under-eye area (1 minute)** — Use the smallest cup with very light suction. Glide from the inner corner of the eye outward along the orbital bone. Do not pull the cup directly under the eye. Spend 30 seconds per side. If you have thin skin under the eyes, reduce suction further or skip this step.

## Frequency

If you are new to facial cupping, start with 3 sessions per week for the first two to three weeks. This gives your skin time to adapt to the new stimulation. After this adjustment period, you can gradually increase to daily practice if your skin tolerates it well. Some users prefer 5-6 sessions per week with one rest day, while others maintain a consistent 3-4 sessions per week and still see excellent results. The right frequency is the one your skin responds to best — pay attention to how your skin feels and adjust accordingly. If you notice persistent redness or sensitivity, reduce frequency until your skin recovers.
