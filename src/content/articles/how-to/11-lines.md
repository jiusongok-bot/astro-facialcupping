The vertical lines between the eyebrows — often called 11 lines — are one of the most common concerns facial cupping practitioners address. These dynamic wrinkles form from repeated expression movements and respond well to the muscle-relaxing and circulation-boosting effects of cupping.

## Understanding 11 Lines

11 lines are caused by the repeated contraction of the corrugator and procerus muscles when you frown, concentrate, or squint. Over time, the skin in this area loses elasticity, and the lines become etched even when the face is at rest. Facial cupping helps by relaxing the underlying muscles while increasing blood flow to support skin repair.

## Technique

Start with clean, dry skin and apply a generous amount of facial oil. Use the smallest cup from your set — this concentrates suction on the target area without pulling surrounding skin.

Place the cup at the center of the frown area, just above the nose, with very light suction. Glide upward along the forehead in slow, straight strokes. Repeat 5-8 times. Then work horizontally across the lines, from the center outward, using short gliding motions. Keep the movement slow and steady — rushing can cause the cup to drag.

## Frequency

Treat the 11-line area for no more than 2-3 minutes per session, 3-4 times per week. The skin between the brows is thin and sensitive — overworking it can cause temporary redness or bruising. If you notice any marks, reduce suction or frequency.
