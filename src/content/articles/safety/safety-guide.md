Facial cupping is generally safe when practiced correctly. However, like any physical technique that manipulates the skin and underlying tissues, it comes with guidelines that should be followed to avoid injury or adverse effects. This guide covers the essential do's and don'ts to keep your practice safe and effective.

## Do's

### Always Use Oil or Serum for Glide

Facial cupping requires a slippery surface to move smoothly. Without adequate lubrication, the cups will drag against the skin, causing discomfort and potentially damaging the skin barrier. Use a high-quality facial oil, serum, or moisturizer that allows the cups to glide effortlessly.

### Start with Light Suction

Silicone cups give you full control over suction pressure. Begin with the lightest squeeze possible — just enough to create a seal. You can always increase suction as you become more experienced, but starting too strong can cause bruising or broken capillaries.

### Keep Cups Moving

Never hold a facial cup in one place. Unlike body cupping where stationary cups are part of the treatment, facial cupping relies on continuous gliding movements. Pausing in one spot concentrates suction on a small area, which can lead to marks or tissue damage.

### Clean Cups Before and After Each Use

Your cups come into direct contact with oil, dead skin cells, and bacteria from your face. Wash them thoroughly with warm water and mild soap after every use. Allow them to dry completely before storing. For extra hygiene, you can boil silicone cups for a few minutes or wipe them with alcohol.

### Stop If You Experience Pain

Facial cupping should never hurt. A gentle pulling sensation is normal, but sharp pain, burning, or stinging means something is wrong. Release the suction immediately and check your technique, glide, and suction pressure before continuing.

## Don'ts

### Don't Use on Broken or Irritated Skin

Avoid cupping over sunburn, cuts, scrapes, eczema, rosacea flare-ups, or any area where the skin barrier is compromised. Cupping on damaged skin can worsen irritation and delay healing.

### Don't Use on Active Acne

Cupping over inflamed acne lesions can spread bacteria and increase inflammation. If you have active breakouts, work around them or wait until your skin has calmed down before cupping.

### Don't Apply Strong Suction

The skin on your face is thin and delicate. Strong suction that might be appropriate for the back or shoulders can cause broken capillaries, bruising, and tissue damage on the face. Gentle suction is all that is needed for effective treatment.

### Don't Leave Cups Stationary

Stationary suction on the face can quickly lead to bruising and circular marks. Always keep the cups moving in smooth, deliberate patterns — typically outward and upward following the natural contours of the face.

### Don't Use Body Cupping Sets on the Face

Body cups are too large and create excessive suction for facial use. Always use cups specifically designed for facial cupping. These are smaller, softer, and give you the fine control needed for safe facial treatment.

## When to Consult a Professional

If you have a medical condition affecting your skin, circulation, or healing, consult a healthcare provider before starting facial cupping. This includes individuals on blood-thinning medications, those with active skin infections, and anyone with a history of vascular disorders.
