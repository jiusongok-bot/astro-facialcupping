One of the most frequently asked questions about facial cupping is whether it can be practiced daily. The answer depends on your experience level, technique, and skin sensitivity.

![Facial cupping massage](/images/articles/cupping-massage.jpg)

## For Beginners

If you are new to facial cupping, start with 2-3 times per week. This allows your skin to adapt to the practice and gives you time to refine your technique without overstimulating your skin.

## For Experienced Practitioners

With proper technique — light suction, adequate oil, and consistent movement — daily facial cupping is generally safe for most people. Many experienced practitioners incorporate a 5-minute daily session into their morning routine.

## Signs You're Overdoing It

If you experience any of these signs, reduce frequency:
- Persistent redness lasting more than 30 minutes
- Skin tenderness between sessions
- Visible marks or discoloration
- Increased skin sensitivity

## Recommended Frequency Guide

| Experience Level | Frequency | Session Length |
|----------------|-----------|---------------|
| Beginner (first month) | 2-3 times per week | 5 minutes |
| Intermediate | 4-5 times per week | 5-10 minutes |
| Advanced | Daily | 5-10 minutes |

## Listen to Your Skin

The most important rule is to pay attention to how your skin responds. If your skin feels good and shows no signs of irritation, daily practice can be beneficial. If you notice sensitivity or marks, reduce frequency.
