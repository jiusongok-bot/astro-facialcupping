Rena Chris has become a popular choice on Amazon for affordable facial cupping sets. But does lower price mean lower quality? We tested their set against premium brands to find out whether the savings come with meaningful trade-offs.

## What You Get

The Rena Chris facial cupping set typically includes 4 silicone cups in different sizes (small, medium, large, and extra large), a travel storage pouch, and an illustrated instruction guide. The cups range from approximately 15 mm to 40 mm in diameter, providing adequate coverage for most facial areas including the under-eye region, cheeks, jawline, and forehead. The set also includes a bonus e-book with technique guidance.

## Build Quality

The silicone used by Rena Chris is food-grade and BPA-free, as stated on the packaging and product listing. While not certified medical-grade like some premium brands (Bellabaci, Primally Pure), the quality is adequate for home use for most users. The cups have a smooth finish and consistent wall thickness. In our testing, the cups performed well across 20+ sessions with no visible degradation. The smallest cup is appropriate for the under-eye area, though it requires careful suction control since smaller cups concentrate pressure into a smaller surface area.

The main quality difference compared to premium brands is in the feel of the silicone — it is slightly less flexible than medical-grade alternatives, and the edge finishing, while smooth, is not as refined as higher-priced competitors. These differences are noticeable in direct comparison but unlikely to affect results for most home users.

## Suction Performance

The cups create a reliable seal and maintain suction well during gliding movements. The different sizes allow for targeted treatment of various face areas. The largest cup works well for cheeks and forehead, the medium cups handle jawline and temples, and the small cup is appropriate for the under-eye area. The squeeze-and-release mechanism is responsive, and the cups hold suction without needing frequent re-squeezing during a session. The texture on the cup grip is sufficient for control even with oil on the hands.

## Value Proposition

At a fraction of the price of premium brands (typically $10-15), the Rena Chris set offers excellent value for beginners who want to try facial cupping without a significant investment. The quality-to-price ratio is strong — you get functional, safe cups that perform their primary job effectively. The main trade-offs are the lack of medical-grade certification and the less refined finish compared to premium sets.

## Verdict

The Rena Chris facial cupping set is an excellent entry-level option for beginners. While it may not match the material certification or refined feel of premium brands, the practical performance is solid for home use. For most users starting their facial cupping journey, this set provides everything needed to begin practicing effectively. If you find that you enjoy the practice and want to upgrade later, the low entry cost makes this a low-risk starting point.
