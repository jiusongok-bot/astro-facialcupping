Beyond traditional cupping sets, several categories of tools can enhance your facial cupping practice. Here is a look at the options available and what each is best suited for.

## Individual Cups

Some practitioners prefer buying individual cups in specific sizes rather than pre-packaged sets. This allows you to select exactly the sizes you need and replace worn cups individually instead of replacing an entire set. Individual cups are widely available from brands like Bellabaci, Primally Pure, and generic medical supply retailers. Prices range from $3 to $10 per cup depending on size and brand.

## Electric Cupping Devices

Electric facial cupping devices automate the suction process using battery-powered pumps. These typically offer adjustable suction levels with preset modes for different face areas. The advantage is consistent, repeatable pressure — useful if you struggle with manual suction control. The downsides are higher cost ($50-$150), reliance on batteries or charging, and more parts to clean. Popular options include the NuFace and LE MASSAGE brands.

## Gua Sha and Cupping Combos

Some tool manufacturers combine facial cupping with gua sha in multi-tool kits. These bundles typically include one or two silicone cups alongside a gua sha stone or stainless steel scraping tool. While convenient for beginners wanting to explore both techniques, dedicated tools for each practice typically perform better than combo kits where both tools may be entry-level quality.
