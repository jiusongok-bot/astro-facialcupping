Facial cupping is a non-invasive beauty and wellness technique that uses small suction cups on the face to stimulate blood flow, promote lymphatic drainage, and encourage collagen production.

Unlike traditional body cupping, which often leaves circular marks, facial cupping uses lighter suction and constant movement to avoid bruising. The practice draws from ancient cupping therapy traditions found in Chinese, Egyptian, and Middle Eastern medicine.

![Facial cupping glass cup](/images/articles/cupping-glass.jpg)

## How It Differs from Body Cupping

Facial cups are smaller, softer, and typically made from silicone rather than glass or bamboo. The suction is gentler, and practitioners keep the cups in constant motion across the face rather than leaving them stationary.

## Why People Practice It

People turn to facial cupping for a variety of reasons including reducing facial puffiness, minimizing the appearance of fine lines, improving skin tone, and promoting overall relaxation.
