## Issues Found

### Critical (Immediate Action Required)

1. **C-01: Author bylines hidden** — Authors exist in frontmatter but `ArticleLayout` never renders `entry.data.author`. Google and users see anonymous content. On a YMYL-adjacent topic (skincare/health), this is a ranking liability.

2. **C-02: Zero external citations** — Not a single outbound link to a published study, medical authority, or expert source across 53 articles. The research/evidence.md references "a 2018 review in the Journal of Traditional and Complementary Medicine" but doesn't link to it. This destroys AI citation readiness and E-E-A-T.

3. **C-03: Average 290 words per article** — The average article takes 60-90 seconds to read. For a site claiming to be "the most comprehensive resource," the content depth is critically insufficient.

### High Priority

4. **C-04: Section landing pages are empty** — Pages like `/benefits/`, `/safety/`, `/tools/` have zero introductory content. Only a title and an article card grid. No context, no overview, no H2 content.

5. **C-05: No publication dates visible** — `datePublished` and `dateUpdated` exist in frontmatter and schema but the `ArticleLayout` doesn't display them to users. Content freshness is invisible.

6. **C-06: Most articles attributed to "Team"** — 45/53 articles have `author: "FacialCupping.com Team"`. On a topic where readers need to trust the source, low-credibility collective authorship fails the "Who" test.

7. **C-07: Pervasive AI-content structural pattern** — Every article follows the identical template. This is detectable as a low-quality signal. Human-written content has variation in structure, depth, and voice.

### Medium Priority

8. **C-08: No original media** — All images are stock photography. No original before/after photos, no demonstration videos, no infographics, no charts or data visualizations.

9. **C-09: No customer testimonials or social proof** — Zero real user experiences, reviews, or testimonials despite claims that "many users report positive results."

10. **C-10: GlowCup articles are especially thin** — The GlowCup brand section (story, philosophy, standards, partnership, contact) averages ~170 words per article. This is the thinnest section and represents the site's own brand.

11. **C-11: Missing FAQPage schema** — FAQ content exists in `/learn/faq` and `/safety/faq` but uses no structured data. (Note: FAQ rich results retired May 2026, but schema still aids AI parsing.)

12. **C-12: No author bio or credentials page** — Even where real authors exist (Sarah Chen, James Liu), there is no mechanism for readers to verify their credentials. No LinkedIn, no license number, no clinic affiliation.

### Low Priority

13. **C-13: Related links use relative paths with hardcoded sections** — Related links at article bottoms use hardcoded section paths. Prone to breakage if content is restructured.

14. **C-14: No "last updated" visible on articles** — Despite `dateUpdated` being in the schema, there's no human-readable "Updated June 2026" badge.

## Recommendations

### Immediate Fixes (can be done in < 2 hours)

1. **Show author bylines in ArticleLayout** — Render `{entry.data.author}` in the article header. If "FacialCupping.com Team", link to `/about/editorial` to explain who the team is.

2. **Show publication/update dates** — Add `<time>` elements displaying `datePublished` and `dateUpdated` to every article.

### Short-Term (1-2 weeks)

3. **Add external citations to all research/evidence articles** — Link to actual PubMed studies, journal articles, and authoritative sources. Start with the 3 research articles (evidence.md, experts.md, studies.md).

4. **Write section landing page introductions** — Add 150-300 words of descriptive H2 content above the article grid on each of the 12 section pages. Use a reusable `<SectionIntro>` component.

5. **Create author bio system** — Add an `author` content collection or data file with full bios for Sarah Chen (credentials, experience, clinic) and James Liu (TCM qualifications, years of practice). Display a compact author box at the bottom of each article.

6. **Replace stock images with original media** — At minimum, produce original before/after photos and technique demonstration photos. Even smartphone-quality images are better than generic stock.

### Medium-Term (1-3 months)

7. **Deepen the 10 thinnest articles first** — Priority list: contact.md, editorial.md, review-process.md, glowcup/* (all 5), oils/best-oil.md, brands/bellabaci.md, brands/primally-pure.md, brands/rena-chris.md, tools/best-tools.md, benefits/rejuvenation.md. Expand each to 800+ words.

8. **Rewrite the "Team" articles with named authors** — If Sarah Chen or another credentialed writer can claim authorship, reassign. If not, add a transparent statement: "This article was researched and written by the FacialCupping.com editorial team and reviewed by [Name], [Credentials]."

9. **Add real case studies** — Document actual facial cupping journeys with consent. "Sarah, 34, used facial cupping 4x/week for 8 weeks. Here's what changed." Include real photos.

10. **Create comparison tables for multi-article topics** — E.g., a master comparison table across all brands, all oils, all tools. This is highly citable by AI systems.

### Long-Term (3-6 months)

11. **Target 1,000+ words for pillar articles** — The core articles (what-is-facial-cupping, benefits-overview, safety-guide, evidence-review) should be 1,500-2,500 words with comprehensive coverage.

12. **Add FAQPage / HowTo structured data** — Article schema exists. Add FAQPage for FAQ articles and HowTo for tutorial articles to improve AI parsing.

13. **Build topical authority clusters** — Each section should function as a content cluster with the section page as the pillar and articles as supporting content, linked with clear topic-relationship markup.

14. **Collect and display user testimonials** — Real user experiences with facial cupping build social proof and enhance the Experience E-E-A-T signal.

### Quick Wins for AI Citation Readiness

1. Extract key statistics from research.md into a standalone `<table>` with data points AI systems can cite
2. Add clear Q&A formatting to FAQ articles (question in H3, answer in paragraph)
3. Link every "study" mention to the actual PubMed URL
4. Add HowTo schema to the tutorial and routine articles

## Summary

The site has a **solid structural foundation** (12 sections, 53 articles, clear taxonomy, correct schema, good CMS design) but the **content itself is critically thin** — averaging ~290 words per article with zero external citations, hidden author credentials, and a detectable AI-generative pattern. The E-E-A-T profile scores low across all four dimensions.

The good news: most issues are fixable content edits rather than technical re-architecture. Displaying existing frontmatter data (author, dates), adding external links, and deepening the thinnest articles would move the score from 34 to ~55-60 within a month of focused work.
