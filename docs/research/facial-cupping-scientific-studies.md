| Criterion | Rating | Notes |
|-----------|--------|-------|
| **Experience** | ❌ Weak | Author is "FacialCupping.com Team" — not an identifiable person with relevant credentials |
| **Expertise** | ⚠️ Moderate | Cites 5 peer-reviewed sources (BMJ Open, Journal of Pain, Frontiers, Skin Research & Technology, Back Musculoskeletal Rehabilitation). Discusses pressures, durations, dosages. But no named expert interpretation. |
| **Authoritativeness** | ⚠️ Moderate | Sources are from reputable journals. However, the site is a commercial .com (not .edu/.org) and the article is not authored by a named researcher or medical professional. |
| **Trustworthiness** | ✅ Strong | Transparent about limitations: "whether the circulation-boosting effects scale proportionally at lower pressures remains an open question" and "dedicated facial cupping trials are still limited." No overblown claims. |

### Readability
- Flesch-Kincaid estimated: Grade 11–13 (appropriate for educated general audience)
- Clear section breaks, text is well-structured
- Plain language explanations (e.g., "increased perfusion is thought to promote oxygenation")
- No jargon without context — good

### Thin Content Assessment
- **~450 words** — well below the 1,500–2,000 word threshold expected for a YMYL research pillar page
- Each section averages ~120–150 words — barely more than a paragraph each
- "Cupping and Pain Management" section: only 3 sentences — could be expanded with specific study findings, effect sizes, and quality assessments
- "Limitations of Current Evidence": the most important section for trustworthiness, yet only ~80 words — should be the most substantial section

### Duplicate Content Risk
- **Significant overlap** with `facial-cupping-evidence-review.md`:
  - Both cite the same 2021 circulation study (DOI 10.1111/srt.12970) and 2021 dose-response study (DOI 10.3389/fbioe.2020.608509)
  - Both cite the same 2020 meta-analysis (DOI 10.1016/j.jpain.2020.01.002) and 2018 BMJ Open review
  - Both have a "Limitations" section discussing similar concerns (small samples, lack of facial-specific trials)
- **Risk: High** — these pages are positioned as separate articles but share too much source material. They need clearer differentiation. The scientific-studies page should focus on **individual study details** (methodology, sample size, pressure levels, specific outcomes), while evidence-review should focus on the **overall evidence landscape and ratings**.

### What Works Well
- Excellent transparent framing: "most circulation studies use body-cupping suction levels... considerably stronger than the gentle suction used in facial cupping" — this is a strong trust signal
- Specific numerical data: "5 to 17 times baseline," "-225 to -300 mmHg," "-50 to -100 mmHg"
- Commitment to update: "We track new publications monthly and update this summary as the evidence base grows"

### Issues

| Issue | Severity | Detail |
|-------|----------|--------|
| Generic `Article` type instead of `ScholarlyArticle` | Medium | Research content about scientific studies should use `ScholarlyArticle` for richer SERP features and better semantic clarity |
| No `about` property | Medium | Should reference `MedicalTherapy` or `MedicalCondition` to link to medical ontology |
| No `citation` array | Medium | The 5 DOI sources should be marked up as `citation` properties in JSON-LD for citation retrieval by Google Scholar and AI systems |
| `author` is `Organization` not `Person` | Low | Acceptable for team-authored content, but weaker for E-E-A-T |
| No `reviewedBy` or `editor` | Low | Adding a medical reviewer credential would strengthen E-E-A-T signal |

### Recommended Schema
```json
{
  "@context": "https://schema.org",
  "@type": "ScholarlyArticle",
  "headline": "Facial Cupping Scientific Studies",
  "description": "Key published studies on cupping therapy summarized for a general audience.",
  "about": {
    "@type": "MedicalTherapy",
    "name": "Cupping Therapy"
  },
  "citation": [
    { "@type": "ScholarlyArticle", "name": "Cupping increases skin blood flow", "identifier": "10.1111/srt.12970" },
    { "@type": "ScholarlyArticle", "name": "Dose-response study of cupping", "identifier": "10.3389/fbioe.2020.608509" },
    { "@type": "ScholarlyArticle", "name": "Cupping for pain: meta-analysis", "identifier": "10.1016/j.jpain.2020.01.002" },
    { "@type": "ScholarlyArticle", "name": "Cupping for neck pain: systematic review", "identifier": "10.1136/bmjopen-2018-021070" }
  ]
}
```
